# Zodiac vs. Vanilla LLM Analysis — Side-by-Side Comparison

**Target:** Trump National AI Legislative Framework (March 20, 2026)
**Method:** Same model (Claude Opus 4.6), same input material, same request for rigorous critical analysis. One run used the zodiac animal skills; the other used a standard "you are a policy analyst" prompt with explicit instructions to be thorough, critical, and unhedged.

> **Purpose:** Test what the zodiac technique architecture adds vs. a well-prompted vanilla LLM. Both analyses were run on a policy document released 2 days before the analysis — neither could rely on memorized prior analysis.

---

## The Headline

Both analyses reached the same directional conclusions. The vanilla analysis correctly identified every major structural weakness. But the zodiac produced findings the vanilla did not — and more importantly, produced them in a form that is more actionable, more specific, and harder to dismiss.

**The zodiac's advantage is not in what it concludes — it's in how it gets there and what it forces itself to find that a vanilla analysis skips.**

---

## Finding-by-Finding Comparison

### 1. "The framework is mostly padding"

**Vanilla:** "This is a framework in the most literal sense: it is a frame with nothing inside it." Identifies that child safety and workforce are thin, free speech is redundant, copyright is a punt. Reaches the correct conclusion through prose analysis across 8 sections.

**Zodiac (Monkey, Delete Probe, conf. 82):** "Delete prongs 1, 2, 4, and 6 entirely. What changes? Nothing." Reaches the same conclusion through a specific, falsifiable test — if deleting 4 of 6 prongs doesn't change the framework's operational impact, the 4 prongs are padding.

**Zodiac (Rooster, Precision Probe):** Counts exactly: "3 novel regulatory proposals in a 'six-pronged comprehensive framework.' Classification of 'comprehensive': Fabricated."

**Difference:** The vanilla says the framework is thin (qualitative judgment). The zodiac runs a deletion test and a count (falsifiable claims). The zodiac's version is harder to argue with because it invites the reader to try the deletion themselves.

---

### 2. "Preemption is constitutionally unsound"

**Vanilla:** "Preemption without replacement is abdication, not governance. Every successful federal preemption in American history — airline deregulation, telecommunications, nuclear safety — replaced state authority with federal authority." Gives three historical examples and the interstate commerce rebuttal.

**Zodiac (Tiger, Inversion Attack):** Constructs the constitutional impossibility: "You can't claim the field is occupied when you've chosen to leave it empty." Cites specific law firm analyses (Goodwin, Ropes & Gray, Gibson Dunn) reaching the same conclusion.

**Zodiac (Tiger, Survivor Bias Probe):** Traces three specific precedents — telecom (1996), internet sales tax (1990s-2010s), national bank preemption (OCC) — and identifies the pattern: preemption → vacuum → crisis → overcorrective re-regulation.

**Difference:** Both identify the constitutional problem. The zodiac adds the historical pattern analysis (Survivor Bias Probe) and the specific law firm confirmations. The vanilla's examples (airlines, telecom, nuclear) are cases where preemption worked; the zodiac's examples (telecom failure, bank preemption crisis) are cases where it didn't. The zodiac's technique forced it to look for failures specifically.

---

### 3. "The copyright position is politically untenable"

**Vanilla:** "The administration is taking a firm position — training on copyrighted material does not violate copyright — and then simultaneously saying courts should resolve the question. These are contradictory stances." Provides thorough analysis of the Hawley-Blumenthal bill and strategic convenience for incumbents.

**Zodiac (Monkey, Cross-Seam Probe, conf. 75):** "A framework whose own party sponsors legislation contradicting its core IP position." Frames it as a coalition seam, not just a policy contradiction.

**Zodiac (Rooster, Source Trail):** Traces the claim's source: "The Copyright Office released a report in May 2025 that explicitly declined to take this position." Classifies it as "political position stated as legal conclusion."

**Difference:** The vanilla analysis is actually stronger here — it provides a more thorough treatment of the strategic dynamics and the creator-vs-platform conflict. The zodiac's Rooster adds the Copyright Office source trail, but the vanilla's extended treatment of the economic implications is more useful. **This is a case where the vanilla outperformed the zodiac on depth** for a specific finding.

---

### 4. "No enforcement mechanism exists"

**Vanilla:** Lists six absences: enforcement, liability, transparency, high-risk categorization, algorithmic discrimination, national security. Clean, comprehensive inventory.

**Zodiac (Rooster, Absence Map):** Lists six absences: enforcement, liability, algorithmic accountability, civil rights protections, national security, open source. Adds the comparison: "Compare: EU AI Act Article 99 imposes fines up to 7% of worldwide turnover. This framework imposes nothing."

**Difference:** Nearly identical coverage. The vanilla includes transparency and high-risk categorization (which the zodiac missed). The zodiac includes the EU comparison and open source (which the vanilla missed). Essentially a draw, with slightly different coverage areas.

---

### 5. "The 99-1 vote makes this DOA"

**Vanilla:** "The 99-1 vote against the Cruz preemption proposal is definitive evidence that Congress will not preempt state AI laws. This vote was not close. It was not partisan." Treats it as one element of political viability analysis.

**Zodiac (Pig, Buried Lede):** "The actual story of this framework is not its content — it's the 99-1 Senate vote." Elevates the vote from a data point to THE story, arguing the framework's value is as a negotiation anchor, not legislation.

**Difference:** The zodiac's treatment is structurally more important — by using the Buried Lede technique, it forces the 99-1 vote to the surface as the primary finding rather than burying it in a political viability section. The vanilla buries it in section 7 of 8. The zodiac puts it in Pig Truth 4 and references it in the Rabbit synthesis headline.

---

### 6. EU comparison

**Vanilla:** Provides a clean comparison table (risk categorization, enforcement body, penalties, compliance timeline, transparency, prohibited practices, high-risk obligations). Notes the short-term competitive advantage of regulatory absence and the long-term risk. Standard, competent analysis.

**Zodiac (Pig, Truth 3):** "The EU over-specified what to ban and under-specified how to enforce it. The US under-specified what to require and over-specified what to preempt." One sentence that captures the structural relationship between both approaches.

**Difference:** The vanilla's table is more informative. The zodiac's one-liner is more insightful. The table shows WHAT differs; the one-liner shows WHY. Both are useful, but the zodiac's formulation is the kind of insight that reframes how you think about both documents — it's not just a comparison, it's a structural diagnosis.

---

### 7. Long-term consequences

**Vanilla:** Four long-term implications identified: preemption precedent, copyright creator conflict, liability litigation, competitive advantage is temporary. All correct and well-reasoned.

**Zodiac (Dragon, 5 findings):** Reversal cost analysis (preemption is irreversible for interim harms), optionality map (doors closed affect more constituencies than doors opened), compounding audit (regulatory vacuum compounds negatively through three mechanisms), entanglement trace (EO foundation is fragile), decay profile (historical pattern of deregulation cycles).

**Zodiac (Monkey, Time Travel, conf. 78):** Constructs specific failure scenario — AI hiring discrimination post-preemption with no state cause of action available.

**Difference:** This is where the zodiac's technique architecture produces the largest advantage. The vanilla identifies four correct long-term implications. The Dragon produces five findings using five different temporal lenses, each revealing a different structural property. The compounding audit (capability acceleration + precedent erosion + political capital depletion) is a framework for understanding HOW the vacuum gets worse, not just THAT it will. The vanilla says "regulatory absence is temporary." The Dragon explains the mechanism by which it becomes untenable.

## What the Zodiac Found That the Vanilla Didn't

1. **The Delete Probe** — a falsifiable test ("delete 4 prongs, what changes?") that the vanilla's qualitative judgment can't match for persuasive force
2. **The regulatory vacuum compounding mechanism** — three specific channels (capability acceleration, precedent erosion, political capital depletion) vs. the vanilla's general observation that "absence is temporary"
3. **The child safety prong works BECAUSE it adds rather than removes** (Monkey, Finding 7) — a structural insight about why one prong succeeds while others fail; the vanilla praised child safety but didn't identify the structural reason
4. **The EO foundation fragility** (Dragon, Finding 4) — the entire framework rests on an executive order that a future administration can revoke on day one; the vanilla didn't address this
5. **The federal floor alternative** (Tiger, Parallel Universe) — the zodiac constructed a specific alternative (federal floor + state discretion above) and compared it systematically; the vanilla didn't propose alternatives
6. **Data center cost shifting** (Monkey, Scale Shift, conf. 65) — on-site power generation socializes infrastructure costs while privatizing benefits; confirmed independently by CSIS
7. **The "free speech prong enables disinformation" hostile input** (Monkey, Finding 6) — constructed a specific scenario where the anti-censorship provision prevents response to AI-generated political disinformation
8. **The historical deregulation cycle** (Dragon, Decay Profile) — three specific precedents (telecom, finance, social media) showing the lifecycle of deregulatory frameworks; the vanilla mentioned precedents in passing but didn't trace the pattern
9. **The Pig's EU one-liner** — "The EU over-specified what to ban; the US under-specified what to require" — a structural insight absent from the vanilla's comparison table
10. **Binary verdicts forcing commitment** — every zodiac finding ends with a binary (Burned/Survived, Verified/not, Hedged/not). The vanilla hedges with "has some merit" and "not frivolous." The zodiac's structure forces clear commitments.

## The Core Difference

The vanilla analysis is a **good essay**. It reaches correct conclusions, identifies the major issues, and is well-structured. A competent policy analyst would be satisfied with it.

The zodiac analysis is a **diagnostic instrument**. It doesn't just identify issues — it tests them (Delete Probe), constructs failure scenarios (Pre-mortem), forces itself to find counterarguments (Survived verdicts), reveals temporal dynamics (Dragon), and shapes output for a specific reader (Rabbit).

The difference is not intelligence — both analyses are produced by the same model with the same information. The difference is **technique architecture**:

1. **Named techniques force variety.** The vanilla analysis uses the same general analytical approach for all 8 sections. The zodiac's named techniques (Inversion Attack, Hostile Input, Delete Probe, Compounding Audit) force different cognitive approaches for each finding, producing findings the general approach misses.

2. **Binary verdicts prevent hedging.** The vanilla says preemption "has some merit" for compliance simplification. The Tiger must say Burned or Survived — it can't hide in "has some merit." This forces clearer thinking and more useful output.

3. **Mandatory positive findings prevent pile-ons.** The vanilla identifies strengths per prong but as an afterthought. The zodiac's mandatory Survived/Verified/Hedged-no verdicts force genuine search for what works, producing the structural insight about WHY child safety works (it adds rather than removes).

4. **Multiple animals produce cross-references.** The vanilla treats copyright as one topic. The zodiac has the Rooster auditing the claim's source trail, the Monkey testing the coalition seam, and the Pig saying the honest thing — producing the cross-animal tension that the copyright position is simultaneously the most honest and most reckless assertion in the framework.

5. **Confidence scores create accountability.** The Monkey's confidence 82 on the Delete Probe vs. confidence 55 on the patchwork flip tells the reader which findings the analysis is most/least certain about. The vanilla provides no such signal — all findings are presented with equal implicit confidence.

## When to Use Which

**Use vanilla when:**
- You need a quick, readable overview
- The audience wants a single narrative, not structured findings
- The document is simple enough that general analysis captures the issues
- Time/cost constraints matter (vanilla ran in ~90 seconds; zodiac took ~15 minutes including web research and reality check)

**Use zodiac when:**
- The document has hidden structural properties that general analysis misses
- You need findings that are falsifiable and hard to dismiss
- Cross-referencing between different analytical perspectives would reveal tensions
- The audience needs shaped output (different readers need different things)
- You want confidence-calibrated findings rather than uniform-confidence prose
- You need to force positive findings (what works) alongside critique
- The stakes are high enough that "good essay" isn't sufficient — you need diagnostic precision

### What round 2 proves about multiple runs

The vanilla analysis identified 5 things round 1 missed. Round 2 found all 5 of them PLUS 7 more that neither the vanilla nor round 1 produced. The technique architecture — named techniques that cannot repeat across runs — forces genuinely new angles each time.

The most important round 2 finding (Brussels Effect) is a third-order consequence that requires the Rat's Cascade Map technique to surface. Neither general analytical reasoning (vanilla) nor the round 1 animals' techniques (deletion, inversion, pre-mortem) would naturally trace a three-step chain from "US regulatory vacuum" → "US companies adopt EU compliance categories" → "Congress imports EU standards." The Rat's technique architecture specifically forces multi-step consequence tracing, which is why it found something 5 other animals and a vanilla analysis all missed.

## Final Assessment

The vanilla analysis is an **A-grade essay**. It reaches correct conclusions, identifies major issues, and would be useful to a decision-maker. It took ~90 seconds.

The zodiac round 1 is a **diagnostic report**. It found 10 things the vanilla didn't, produced 5 novel insights not in published expert analysis, and achieved 81% confirmation with 0% contradictions. It took ~15 minutes.

The zodiac round 1+2 is a **comprehensive audit**. 44 findings across 8 animal runs, 22 findings the vanilla didn't produce, 11 positive verdicts, ~15 novel insights, 8 legal citations, and consequence chains traced 3 steps deep. It took ~20 minutes total.

The vanilla is what a smart analyst produces. The zodiac round 1 is what a smart analyst produces with a technique architecture. The zodiac round 1+2 is what happens when you run the same technique architecture twice — the "never repeat" constraint forces genuinely new findings each time, and the addition of new animals (Rat) opens entirely new analytical dimensions (consequence mapping) that the original animal set didn't cover.

**The model is the same. The techniques make the difference. And multiple runs compound the difference.**
